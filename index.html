Digital Transformation Timeline: HSBC

Stage 1: 2016
The bank undergoes a strategic pivot towards internet and mobile banking inkey markets such as the Uk and Hong Kong, which includes the role out of voice and fingerprint recogniction. It also launches new labs that focus on AI and data management, along side cyber security. This all happens after their 2016 annual report identifes adoption of digital technology by customers as "the most transformative force".
Stage 2: 2017-2018
HSBC sets up a new "digital-first strategy" model in Asian regions that they defined as high growth regions where they set out not only a new digital banking platfrom in the Pearl River Delta, but also piloting WeChat banking and virtual teller machines, and evaluating mobile wallets on a partner-to-partner basis. Additonally, HSBC starts migrating its analytics workloads toward cloud computing services, positioning cloud migration as a strategic decision that enables faster product delivery, faster scaling and better use of data.
Stage 3: 2019-2020
A Huawei Case Study on HSBC revealed that they are stargetgially putting emphasis on "partnership and data at the heart of digitisation". The study looked at how HSBC are further leveraging cloud computing and big data to deliver real-time services, and more intelligent business decisions. Recent Research on what are termed "smart branches" argues that combinaing AI, 5G and cloud servcies enables banks to deploy outlets that are essentially self servicing terminal, which can both help with customer interactions from the bank's standpoint and reduce staff and property cost. (Huawei, 2020).

Building on from 2019, HSBC's Asia and Hong Kong digital plans emphsise mobile as a primary interaction channel, supported by improved mobile journeys, and on-the-move onboarding, all of which rely on modern high-bandwidth, low latency connectivity, and emerging 5G networks.

Stage 4: 2021
HSBC launched and scaled its strategic Time-series and Risk-Factors platform, a cloud native market-risk data and analytics system on Google Cloud that can manage end-to-end risk-data lifecycles. STAR delivers a 50-100 per cent reduction n unit risk calculation costs, enabling more frequent and deatiled risk calcualtion, alongside faster repsonse times. Overall, providing a speed, scalability, and cost efficiency benefit.
Stage 5: 2022
HSBC majorly scaled its infrastructure in 2022. Google Cloud case material reports over 100 petabytes of data being transferred into cloud environments, enabling HSBC to increase its compute power and undertake more advanced modelling and anti-crime analytics. Furthermore, this migration strengthens HSBC's data maturity profile as data becomes more accessible and available for AI and analytics. This move also strengthens HSBC's governance and security as encryption and monitoring is diversified across multuple regualtors and regions.
